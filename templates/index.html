Welcome to I-PIM!

Interactive Pedestrian Injury Mapper

Mapping tool

Welcome to I-PIM, a crowd-sourcing research project from Columbia University that will help to reduce the number of pedestrian injuries and fatalities cause by car collisions. We hope to understand what aspects of streets and intersections put pedestrians at risk of being hit by automobiles. We would like you to use a mapping tool to provide us with information about how and where you were injured while out walking. We would like you to place a pin at the location where you were hit by a car and use the drawing tool to draw the route your were walking up to the place where you were injured. You can also write in text to tell us more about what happened when you were hit by the vehicle. Press the below button to start.

Start I-PIM

Step 1

Use the mouse to place the pin at the place where you were hit by a vehicle.

Step 2

Use the pencil tool to draw lines on the map showing us the streets you walked along before you were hit.

Step 3

Please tell us a little more about yourself as well as the accident. If you are a signed up user, your information is already stored.
